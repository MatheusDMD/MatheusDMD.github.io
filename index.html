ScaryVR

A Virtual Reality thriller that makes 3 rooms and 5 minutes seem like forever


You are lost in a small room, old, dark… silent. It reminds you of a real place, it has furniture and the size gives you claustrophobic feelings. You have to get out. But the sounds in your surrounding won't encourage you to even cross the first door. When you reach for the key on top of the old desk… Scared yet? This kind of experience made me interested in further developing the project and getting a deeper understanding of the technology of Virtual Reality. In a team of 2 students, we developed a 5 minute experience for the annual Insper Expo. The goal was to make a immersive experience that would really make you go into a different world, without leaving the room. We achieved that, by creating fun and realistic interactions in a 3 room, free-walking experience, meaning that the metrics weren't only virtual, but they matched the real ones. We developed it using Unity-3D and specifically for the Vive VR HMD.

Neato Hacked - Android app

A vaccum cleaner robot hacked into a self-driving vehicle


My team and I developed Neato (a hacked vacuum cleaner robot) using a ROS (Robot Operating System) tools. Sky-TV (Consulting) - Android app

The 21st century TV-Technitian


Together with SKY-TV, recently bought by AT&T, I have worked at their innovation center to develop a solution to improve TV-technicians efficiency. I was part of developing a feature of their existing app, which was a system where the user could reach technicians and get help as soon as they had an issue. Along with the technical solution application, we developed a simple step-by-step guide to identify and solve the most common problems, reducing cost in one of TVs operators most expensive area. We also created a system, where each technician could go to a moving storage unit to refill and quicker be on their way to the next client in need. During this 4 months consulting program, we were able to deliver a disruptive way to reduce cost and add value to end-users.

L'Oréal Brandstorm 2017

The Challenge


The challenge was to build a product or service inside L'Oréal's Men Expert line that would target the millenial population, which I'm part of. This both made the design process harder as I had to place myself apart from the context to guarantee assertivity on ideas and easier as I could use my connections to get quick insights for the challenge.


During the ideation and prototyping process we analysed and studied the millennial user interaction with not only grooming products and technologies, but what was valued in other products and services, to bring insights and inspiration, and create real disruption in this market. To create our product, we used a lot of social-media concepts and the on-demand trend of personalized products that technologies such as Spotify and Netflix mastered.

L'Oréal Brandstorm 2017

The Project


Only after several iterations of the design and validation process we created a line of personalized grooming products, called UNIQUE. As a Product Service system,through a Chabot conversation (L’Ori), getting a description of needs and tastes regarding the grooming environment, the algorithm creates a personalized set of products for that user. The assigned product is the combination of 3 biodegradable capsules, called PODs. These contain parts of the user’s specific solution, periodcly delivered at their home. Once these capsules are delivered, the user mixes the PODs in their modern-looking dispensers so they can become its unique solution. This solution works for the whole grooming line of Men Expert, having different PODs and Dispensers for shampoo, conditioning, beard and hair care.

IXL - Innovation Olympics

1st Place - Case company: CMI [Fall 2016]


During Fall 2016, I joined the IXL - Innovation Olympics held in Boston. This global competition groups 5 top schools to compete and solve a business case focusing of the companies challenges. Our case company was CMI, the biggest company in Guatemala, to create solutions on food for the Guatemalan base of the pyramid population. To better understand their problems, we visited Brazilian Favelas and talked to people that struggled with food access. We aimed to reduce the cost of livestock production and its sales as a consequence. Given the low access to food in the base of the pyramid due to low income, making food more accessible to anyone in the country. Our goal was to find a way of eradicating famine in Guatemala while at the same time build a profitable solution for the company. We developed a business plan that used insects as source of protein to feed livestock. With it, we were able to reduce production cost in the long term and won the first prize, bringing new insights to CMI.

Insper Hackathon [Spring 2016]

1st Place - Hackathon on Quality of Life


In the first edition of the annual Insper Hackathon we were challenged to create a solution that would improve quality of life (QOL) in Brazil. With such a broad topic our goal was to tackle something that would actually add value to people with low QOL. Our understanding was that the impact would greater if we targeted people with low QOL, rather than otherwise. We developed a platform for financial consulting, with educational strategy to better manage the finance and register expenses in a easy way. With the new knowledge from the education section, the platform give users a better overview of their finance, starting the motivation to make a change, rather than just coming with "advice from experts". With this platform we won Insper's 1st hackathon, and inspired a similar platform from the government tax institution to help the population in debt solve their struggles.
